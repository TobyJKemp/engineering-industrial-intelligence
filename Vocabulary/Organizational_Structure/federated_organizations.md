## One-Sentence Summary
Federated Organizations balance autonomy of semi-independent units with coordination and standardization—enabling local flexibility while maintaining organizational coherence.

## Why This Matters to You
As organizations grow and diversify, federations balance growth and flexibility. Understanding helps you: (1) design organizations that scale, (2) preserve innovation while maintaining standards, (3) coordinate without central control.

## The Core Idea
- **Local autonomy:** Units largely independent
- **Shared standards:** Common standards and guidelines
- **Coordination:** Mechanisms enabling cooperation
- **Central oversight:** Overall direction but not micromanagement

## How It Works
1. **Define unit autonomy:** What decisions local? What central?
2. **Establish standards:** What must be consistent across units?
3. **Create coordination:** How do units work together?
4. **Maintain coherence:** How does overall strategy align?

## Think of It Like This
Federated organizations are like **United States federal system**: States largely autonomous but united by Constitution; coordination on defense, commerce.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Innovation:** Local units innovate
- **Agility:** Faster local decisions
- **Coherence:** Overall strategy maintained
- **Complexity:** Coordination overhead

## Practical Checklist
- [ ] Is unit autonomy defined?
- [ ] Are standards clear?
- [ ] Are coordination mechanisms working?
- [ ] Do units understand overall strategy?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Fragmentation:** Units drift apart; lose coherence.
⚠️ **Coordination overhead:** Too much coordination kills benefit.
