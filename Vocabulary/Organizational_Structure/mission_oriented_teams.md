# Mission-Oriented Teams

## At a Glance
| | |
|---|---|
| **Category** | Team Organization |
| **Complexity** | Intermediate |
| **Time to Learn** | 3-4 hours for concepts |
| **Prerequisites** | Team dynamics, goal setting |

## One-Sentence Summary
Mission-Oriented Teams are organized around a shared mission or outcome—with members focused on achieving mission rather than following procedures or reporting relationships.

## The Core Idea
- **Mission-driven:** Clear mission provides direction
- **Autonomy:** Teams decide how to achieve mission
- **Accountability:** Accountable for mission achievement
- **Flexibility:** Can adapt means to achieve mission

## How It Works
1. **Define mission:** What outcome trying to achieve?
2. **Empower team:** Give team autonomy to achieve mission
3. **Establish metrics:** Clear measure of mission success
4. **Support:** Provide resources needed

## Think of It Like This
Mission-oriented teams are like **SEAL team with objective**: Clear target; team figures out how to achieve; accountability for success.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Motivation:** Mission provides meaning and direction
- **Adaptability:** Can adjust approach to achieve mission
- **Engagement:** People engaged in important mission
- **Accountability:** Clear responsibility

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Mission creep:** Mission changes or expands unexpectedly.
⚠️ **Over-autonomy:** Insufficient oversight creates problems.
⚠️ **Goal misalignment:** Team's interpretation of mission diverges from leadership intent.
⚠️ **Resource misallocation:** Without guidelines, teams may use resources inefficiently.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing mission-oriented teams:
- [ ] Is mission clearly defined and communicated?
- [ ] Are success metrics specific and measurable?
- [ ] Do team members understand mission deeply?
- [ ] Are resources adequate for mission?
- [ ] Is there mechanism for mission clarification if needed?
- [ ] Are constraints on autonomy defined?
- [ ] Is there regular communication with leadership?
- [ ] Can team adapt approach while staying true to mission?
- [ ] Is there support structure for team autonomy?
- [ ] Can you measure mission achievement?
