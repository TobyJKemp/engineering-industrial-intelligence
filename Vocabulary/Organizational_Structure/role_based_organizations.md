# Role-Based Organizations

## At a Glance

## One-Sentence Summary
Role-Based Organizations define clear roles people occupy—with defined responsibilities, authority, and relationships—rather than focusing on individuals or positions.

## The Core Idea
- **Roles defined:** Clear role responsibilities
- **Role authority:** Authority attached to role
- **Role relationships:** Interactions between roles defined
- **Flexibility:** Different people can occupy same role

## How It Works
1. **Define roles:** What roles needed?
2. **Specify responsibilities:** What does each role do?
3. **Establish authority:** What can role decide?
4. **Clarify relationships:** How do roles interact?

## Think of It Like This
Role-based organizations are like **sports team**: Defined positions (pitcher, catcher); different people rotate through positions; role responsibilities constant.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Clarity:** Clear expectations and relationships
- **Flexibility:** Can rotate people through roles
- **Knowledge transfer:** Roles documented; transitions easier
- **Stability:** Focuses on roles, not individuals

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Rigidity:** Roles too fixed; can't adapt.
⚠️ **Individual eclipse:** People feel invisible; roles more important.
⚠️ **Role ambiguity:** Unclear boundaries between roles; overlap and gaps.
⚠️ **Role obsolescence:** Roles defined for past, not future needs.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing role-based organization:
- [ ] Are all necessary roles identified?
- [ ] Are role boundaries clear and non-overlapping?
- [ ] Are role responsibilities documented?
- [ ] Is role authority defined?
- [ ] Are role relationships and interactions clear?
- [ ] Can roles adapt as business needs change?
- [ ] Do people understand their role expectations?
- [ ] Is there mechanism for role negotiation/clarification?
- [ ] Are role transitions managed?
- [ ] Can role approach scale as organization grows?
