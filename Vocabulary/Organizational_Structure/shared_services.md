## One-Sentence Summary
Shared Services consolidate support functions (HR, Finance, IT) into centralized unit serving multiple business units—achieving economies of scale while providing standardized support.

## The Core Idea
- **Centralized:** Support functions in one place
- **Shared:** Serve multiple business units
- **Scale:** Economies of scale reduce cost
- **Standardization:** Consistent processes and standards

## How It Works
1. **Consolidate functions:** Bring support functions together
2. **Establish standards:** Define standard processes
3. **Charge units:** Units pay for services
4. **Optimize:** Continuously improve processes

## Think of It Like This
Shared services are like **printing center for company**: Centralized; serves all departments; cheaper than each department having own printer/staff.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Efficiency:** Cost savings through scale
- **Standardization:** Consistent processes
- **Quality:** Concentrated expertise
- **Responsiveness:** May be slow to respond to specific needs

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Loss of responsiveness:** Centralized service may be slow.
⚠️ **One-size-fits-all:** May not fit all units well.
⚠️ **Quality variation:** Shared service may not match specialized unit capabilities.
⚠️ **Cost transparency:** Unclear whether shared services are truly cheaper.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing shared services:
- [ ] Have you identified services that benefit from consolidation?
- [ ] Is cost analysis showing actual savings?
- [ ] Can service level agreements meet all business units' needs?
- [ ] Is technology infrastructure adequate to serve all units?
- [ ] Can you standardize processes across business units?
- [ ] Is governance clear for shared service?
- [ ] Can units escalate issues if service inadequate?
- [ ] Is there mechanism for continuous improvement?
- [ ] Do business units support consolidation?
- [ ] Can you transition smoothly without service disruption?
