## One-Sentence Summary
Embedded Functions place supporting functions (HR, Finance, IT) within business units rather than centralized—pushing expertise closer to work where it's needed and improving responsiveness.

## Why This Matters to You
Embedded functions affect how quickly organizations respond and how close support is to actual work. Understanding helps you: (1) decide what functions to embed vs. centralize, (2) balance autonomy and standardization, (3) improve support responsiveness.

## The Core Idea
- **Distributed support:** Functions sit within units needing them
- **Local ownership:** Functions report to unit, not central function
- **Closer relationships:** Better understanding of business needs
- **Trade-offs:** Less standardization; more responsiveness

## How It Works
1. **Choose functions to embed:** Finance analysis? HR? Legal? IT?
2. **Organize locally:** Functions report locally, not centrally
3. **Maintain standards:** Central function sets standards
4. **Share learning:** Embedded function shares practices across units

## Think of It Like This
Embedded functions are like **hospital chaplain on surgical unit** vs. centralized: Local and available vs. specialist available by appointment.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Better responsiveness:** Support understands unit's context
- **Faster decisions:** Support embedded in decisions
- **Less standardization:** Each unit operates differently
- **Cost duplication:** Duplicate functions in multiple units

## Practical Checklist
- [ ] Function benefits from being local?
- [ ] Can I maintain standards?
- [ ] Have I addressed cost vs. responsiveness trade-off?
- [ ] Is coordination clear?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Standardization Loss:** Drift from standards if not managed.
⚠️ **Duplication costs:** Expensive to embed everywhere.
⚠️ **Isolation:** Embedded function loses connection to discipline.
