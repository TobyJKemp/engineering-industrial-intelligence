## One-Sentence Summary
Hollywood Model Organizations assemble temporary teams for specific projects—bringing together independent contractors and specialists, then disbanding when project completes.

## The Core Idea
- **Project-based:** Organization forms around projects
- **Temporary teams:** Assemble, work, disband
- **Independent members:** Often contractors, not employees
- **Expertise-driven:** People chosen for specific project needs

## How It Works
1. **Define project:** What needs to be done?
2. **Assemble team:** Bring together contractors/specialists
3. **Execute:** Work on project
4. **Complete:** Disband team when done

## Think of It Like This
Hollywood model is like **film production**: Director hires cinematographer, actors, sound designer; makes film; team disperses.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Flexibility:** Can assemble perfect team for each project
- **Cost efficiency:** Pay only for needed expertise
- **Specialization:** Expert specialists available
- **No continuity:** Team doesn't persist; knowledge lost

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Knowledge loss:** Learning doesn't persist when team disperses.
⚠️ **Overhead:** Administrative overhead of assembling/disbanding.
⚠️ **Coordination complexity:** Managing distributed temporary teams challenging.
⚠️ **Integration friction:** Different teams may have different standards/approaches.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing Hollywood model:
- [ ] Do you have clear project boundaries and timelines?
- [ ] Can you assemble specialized teams for specific projects?
- [ ] Is there mechanism to capture learning between projects?
- [ ] Can you manage contractor logistics efficiently?
- [ ] Do you have standardized contracts and terms?
- [ ] Is there infrastructure to support rapid team assembly/disassembly?
- [ ] Can you maintain quality standards across temporary teams?
- [ ] Is institutional memory preserved beyond team dissolution?
- [ ] Do you have metrics for project success?
- [ ] Is there executive support for this model?
