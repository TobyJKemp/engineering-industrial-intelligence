## One-Sentence Summary
Conversation history is the recorded sequence of exchanges between agents and users, enabling context continuity, coherent responses, and effective dialogue management.

## Why This Matters to You
If you want agents to hold meaningful, multi-turn conversations, conversation history is essential. It supports context continuity, personalization, and advanced dialogue strategies.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Conversation history means:
- Recording all exchanges (user and agent turns)
- Storing and retrieving history for context-aware responses
- Using history to inform future actions or outputs

### What It Isn't
It is not just logging or auditing. True conversation history is structured for use in dialogue management, not just for record-keeping.

## How It Works
1. **Record Exchanges**: Log each turn with timestamps and metadata
2. **Store and Retrieve**: Save history in memory, files, or databases
3. **Leverage for Context**: Use history to inform agent responses and actions

## Think of It Like This
Like a chat transcript that helps you remember what was said and what needs follow-up.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- More coherent and relevant agent responses
- Support for long-running or multi-turn conversations
- Better user experience

**If you don't:**
- Agents forget context and repeat questions
- Fragmented or confusing interactions

## Practical Checklist
- [ ] Is conversation history recorded and structured?
- [ ] Is history accessible for context-aware responses?
- [ ] Is history managed for privacy and efficiency?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Privacy risks with sensitive conversation data
⚠️ History bloat or inefficiency
