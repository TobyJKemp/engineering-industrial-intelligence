## One-Sentence Summary
Workspace structure is the intentional organization of folders, files, and conventions that makes a repository understandable and operable.

## Why This Matters to You
A clear structure is the fastest onboarding tool you can give new contributors. It reduces search time, prevents misplaced artifacts, and clarifies ownership. For AI-assisted workflows, good structure improves retrieval quality and edit safety. In this repository, structure operationalizes the railway metaphor so knowledge flows predictably.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Workspace structure defines where different artifact types belong, how they are named, and how they connect. It includes directory boundaries, templates, and relationship patterns.

Strong structure balances consistency with evolution. It should help contributors find the right place quickly without rigidly blocking useful adaptation.

### What It Isn't
Structure is not cosmetic folder styling. It should reflect operational logic.

It is also not permanent law; structures should evolve as system needs change.

## How It Works
1. Define top-level domains aligned to operational responsibilities.
2. Standardize naming, templates, and placement rules for artifacts.
3. Maintain cross-links and periodic reviews to prevent structural drift.

## Think of It Like This
Think of a rail network map where track layout, station roles, and control zones are designed so traffic can move efficiently and safely.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- Contributors find and place information faster.
- Cross-team collaboration becomes less error-prone.
- Agent context gathering improves with clearer signal paths.

**If you don't:**
- Knowledge fragments into inconsistent locations.
- Maintenance and onboarding costs rise steadily.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Does each top-level area map to a clear operational function?
- [ ] Are templates and naming conventions discoverable and followed?
- [ ] Is there a process to refactor structure when pain points emerge?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Duplicating similar structures without explicit ownership boundaries.
⚠️ Letting ad hoc folders accumulate without review.
