## One-Sentence Summary
Knowledge graph quality refers to the degree to which a knowledge graph is accurate, complete, consistent, up-to-date, and fit for its intended use.

## Why This Matters to You
High-quality knowledge graphs are essential for trustworthy AI, analytics, and automation. Poor quality leads to bad decisions, wasted effort, and loss of trust. As a practitioner, you need to understand, measure, and improve quality to ensure your systems deliver reliable results and value.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Knowledge graph quality encompasses multiple dimensions: accuracy (correctness of facts), completeness (coverage of relevant entities and relationships), consistency (absence of contradictions), timeliness (up-to-date information), and relevance (fit for purpose). Quality is assessed through metrics, validation rules, and sometimes human review.

Improving quality may involve data cleaning, validation, enrichment, and governance processes. Automated tools and manual curation both play a role.

### What It Isn't
Quality is not a single number or a one-time check. It is not just about data volume—more data does not always mean better quality. Quality is not static; it must be maintained as the graph evolves.

## How It Works
1. **Define Quality Criteria:** Identify what matters for your use case.
2. **Measure & Monitor:** Use metrics, validation, and review to assess quality.
3. **Improve & Maintain:** Clean, enrich, and govern the graph over time.

## Think of It Like This
Think of knowledge graph quality like the health of a city: it depends on many factors (cleanliness, safety, infrastructure) and requires ongoing attention.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You build trust in your AI and analytics.
- You reduce errors and rework.
- You deliver more value to users and stakeholders.

**If you don't:**
- Systems may produce unreliable or harmful results.
- Maintenance and troubleshooting become harder.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are quality criteria defined and documented?
- [ ] Are metrics and validation processes in place?
- [ ] Is there a plan for ongoing quality improvement?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Overlooking quality can undermine the entire project.
⚠️ Relying solely on automation may miss subtle issues.
