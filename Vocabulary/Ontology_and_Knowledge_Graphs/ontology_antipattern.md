## One-Sentence Summary
An ontology antipattern is a common modeling mistake or poor practice that leads to ambiguity, inconsistency, or inefficiency in ontologies or knowledge graphs.

## Why This Matters to You
Recognizing and avoiding antipatterns helps you build clearer, more maintainable, and interoperable ontologies. Antipatterns can cause reasoning errors, integration failures, and increased maintenance costs. Learning from these pitfalls improves the quality and reliability of your AI and knowledge systems.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Ontology antipatterns are recurring mistakes in ontology design—such as overloading properties, creating circular references, or using vague class definitions. They are identified through experience, research, and community best practices.

Antipatterns are documented to help practitioners recognize and correct them before they cause problems.

### What It Isn't
An ontology antipattern is not a design pattern (which is a recommended solution). It’s not always obvious—some antipatterns only become apparent as ontologies grow or are integrated with others.

## How It Works
1. **Identify:** Learn about common antipatterns from literature and experience.
2. **Detect:** Review ontologies for signs of antipatterns.
3. **Refactor:** Redesign or correct problematic structures.

## Think of It Like This
Think of ontology antipatterns like bad habits in writing—such as run-on sentences or unclear pronouns—that make your work harder to read and understand.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You build clearer, more robust ontologies.
- You reduce errors and maintenance costs.
- You improve interoperability and reuse.

**If you don't:**
- Ambiguity and errors may go undetected.
- Integration and reasoning become unreliable.
- Maintenance becomes more difficult and costly.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are common antipatterns reviewed and avoided?
- [ ] Is ontology design peer-reviewed or validated?
- [ ] Are corrections documented and shared?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Hidden or subtle antipatterns can persist for years.
⚠️ Fixing antipatterns late can be costly or disruptive.
