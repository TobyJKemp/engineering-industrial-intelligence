## One-Sentence Summary
Ontology diff is the process of identifying, visualizing, and analyzing differences between two versions of an ontology or knowledge graph.

## Why This Matters to You
Tracking changes in ontologies is critical for collaboration, quality assurance, and compliance. Ontology diff tools help you understand what changed, why, and how it affects downstream systems. Without this, errors, regressions, or integration failures can go undetected.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Ontology diff compares two ontology versions—highlighting added, removed, or modified classes, properties, relationships, or axioms. It may use visual tools, reports, or automated scripts to present differences clearly.

Diffing supports review, rollback, and impact analysis in collaborative or regulated environments.

### What It Isn't
Ontology diff is not just a text comparison—it understands the structure and semantics of ontologies. It’s not a substitute for good documentation or governance, but a complement.

## How It Works
1. **Select Versions:** Choose two ontology versions to compare.
2. **Analyze Differences:** Use tools to identify and visualize changes.
3. **Review & Act:** Assess impact, document changes, and update systems as needed.

## Think of It Like This
Think of ontology diff like comparing two blueprints to see what rooms or features have changed in a building renovation.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You catch errors and regressions early.
- You support collaboration and compliance.
- You enable safe, auditable change management.

**If you don't:**
- Changes may go undocumented or cause failures.
- Collaboration and review are hindered.
- Compliance and traceability are weakened.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are ontology versions tracked and documented?
- [ ] Are diff tools or processes in place?
- [ ] Is impact analysis performed for significant changes?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Large or complex ontologies may produce overwhelming diffs.
⚠️ Ignoring semantic changes can miss critical impacts.
