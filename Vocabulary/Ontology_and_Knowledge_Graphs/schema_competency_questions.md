## One-Sentence Summary
Schema competency questions are sample queries or use cases used to validate whether an ontology or schema can answer the intended questions.

## Why This Matters to You
Competency questions ensure that your ontology or schema meets real-world requirements. They guide design, testing, and evaluation, helping you avoid gaps or overengineering.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Competency questions are formulated during ontology development to represent the kinds of queries users or systems should be able to answer. They are used to test and refine the schema.

### What It Isn't
They are not actual queries or code, but natural language questions. They are not a substitute for formal testing, but a complement.

## How It Works
1. **Formulate Questions:** Identify key use cases and express them as questions.
2. **Design Schema:** Ensure the ontology can represent the required information.
3. **Test & Refine:** Attempt to answer the questions using the schema; revise as needed.

## Think of It Like This
Think of competency questions like acceptance criteria for software: they define what "success" looks like for your ontology.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You ensure your ontology meets user needs.
- You catch gaps or ambiguities early.
- You improve design and documentation.


**If you don't:**
- The ontology may fail to answer key questions.
- Gaps or errors may go undetected.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are all key use cases represented as competency questions?
- [ ] Are questions reviewed with stakeholders?
- [ ] Is the schema tested and refined based on these questions?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Vague or overly broad questions are hard to test.
⚠️ Ignoring new requirements can make questions obsolete.
