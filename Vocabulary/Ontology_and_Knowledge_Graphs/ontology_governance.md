## One-Sentence Summary
Ontology governance is the set of processes, roles, and policies that ensure ontologies are developed, maintained, and used effectively across an organization.

## Why This Matters to You
Effective governance ensures ontologies remain accurate, relevant, and aligned with business goals. It reduces risks of inconsistency, duplication, or misuse, and supports compliance, collaboration, and long-term value from knowledge assets.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Ontology governance covers decision-making, change management, quality assurance, and stakeholder engagement for ontologies. It defines who owns, reviews, approves, and updates ontologies, and how changes are tracked and communicated.

### What It Isn't
It is not just technical management or documentation. Governance is not a one-time setup—it’s an ongoing, organization-wide discipline.

## How It Works
1. **Define Roles:** Assign owners, stewards, and reviewers.
2. **Establish Policies:** Set standards for development, review, and change.
3. **Monitor & Improve:** Track usage, gather feedback, and evolve governance as needs change.

## Think of It Like This
Think of ontology governance like city zoning and building codes: it ensures everything is built, maintained, and used according to shared rules and goals.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You maintain high-quality, trusted ontologies.
- You support collaboration and compliance.
- You maximize the value of knowledge assets.

**If you don't:**
- Ontologies may become outdated, inconsistent, or misused.
- Collaboration and integration suffer.
- Compliance and value are at risk.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are roles and responsibilities clearly defined?
- [ ] Are policies documented and enforced?
- [ ] Is there a process for ongoing review and improvement?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Lack of buy-in or unclear roles can undermine governance.
⚠️ Overly rigid policies may stifle innovation.
