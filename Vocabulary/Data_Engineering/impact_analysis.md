## One-Sentence Summary
Impact analysis is the process of assessing the potential effects of a change, event, or decision on systems, data, or business outcomes.

## Why This Matters to You
Impact analysis helps organizations anticipate risks, plan changes, and avoid unintended consequences. It is essential for safe, efficient evolution of AI, data, and business systems. Without it, changes can cause outages, data loss, or compliance failures.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Impact analysis traces dependencies and relationships to determine what will be affected by a proposed change. It uses data lineage, dependency graphs, and scenario modeling to inform decision-making.

### What It Isn't
Impact analysis is not just a checklist; it requires understanding of system interconnections and business context.

It is also not a one-time task—ongoing analysis is needed as systems evolve.

## How It Works
1. Identify the proposed change or event.
2. Trace dependencies and affected components using lineage and documentation.
3. Assess risks, costs, and mitigation strategies.

## Think of It Like This
Think of a railway company evaluating how closing a station will affect schedules, ridership, and revenue across the network.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You reduce risk and avoid costly mistakes.
- You plan changes more effectively.
- You support compliance and business continuity.

**If you don't:**
- Changes may cause outages, data loss, or compliance failures.
- Recovery is slower and more expensive.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are all dependencies and stakeholders identified?
- [ ] Are risks and mitigation strategies documented?
- [ ] Is analysis updated as systems evolve?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Incomplete or outdated documentation.
⚠️ Underestimating indirect or downstream effects.
