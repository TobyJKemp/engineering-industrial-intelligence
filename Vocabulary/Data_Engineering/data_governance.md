## One-Sentence Summary
Data governance is the set of policies, processes, and roles that ensure data is managed, used, and protected effectively across an organization.

## Why This Matters to You
Good governance is essential for compliance, data quality, and trust. It reduces risk, clarifies ownership, and enables responsible data use. In regulated industries, governance is a legal requirement.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Data governance defines who can access, modify, and use data, under what conditions. It includes data stewardship, quality management, privacy, and security controls.

Governance frameworks often include councils, policies, and automated enforcement mechanisms.

### What It Isn't
Governance is not just documentation; it must be operationalized and enforced.

It is also not a one-time project; it requires ongoing attention and adaptation.

## How It Works
1. Define policies, roles, and responsibilities.
2. Implement controls and monitoring.
3. Review and adapt governance as data and regulations evolve.

## Think of It Like This
Think of air traffic control for data—ensuring safe, compliant, and efficient movement.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You reduce compliance risk and improve data quality.
- You clarify ownership and accountability.
- You enable responsible data sharing and innovation.

**If you don't:**
- Data misuse, breaches, and quality issues increase.
- Regulatory penalties and reputational damage become more likely.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are policies and roles clearly defined and communicated?
- [ ] Are controls and monitoring automated where possible?
- [ ] Is governance reviewed and updated regularly?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Governance that is too rigid or too lax.
⚠️ Lack of buy-in from stakeholders.
