# Data Mesh

## At a Glance
| | |

## One-Sentence Summary
Data mesh is a decentralized approach to data architecture that treats data as a product and assigns ownership to domain teams.

## Why This Matters to You
Data mesh enables scalability, agility, and innovation in large organizations. It breaks down data silos and empowers teams to deliver high-quality, discoverable data products. Understanding data mesh is key for modern, federated data platforms.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Data mesh distributes data ownership and responsibility to domain teams, who manage their own pipelines, quality, and governance. It relies on self-serve infrastructure, federated governance, and product thinking.

Mesh architectures support interoperability and standardization across domains.

### What It Isn't
Data mesh is not just a technology stack; it is an organizational and cultural shift.

It is also not a free-for-all—governance and standards are still required.

## How It Works
1. Assign data product ownership to domain teams.
2. Provide self-serve infrastructure and platform capabilities.
3. Enforce federated governance and interoperability standards.

## Think of It Like This
Think of a rail network where each region manages its own lines, but all follow shared safety and interoperability rules.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You scale data delivery and innovation across teams.
- You improve data quality and discoverability.
- You reduce bottlenecks and central team overload.

**If you don't:**
- Centralized bottlenecks and silos persist.
- Data quality and agility suffer.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are domain teams empowered and accountable for data products?
- [ ] Is self-serve infrastructure available?
- [ ] Are governance and interoperability standards enforced?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Lack of buy-in or skills in domain teams.
⚠️ Inconsistent standards and governance.
