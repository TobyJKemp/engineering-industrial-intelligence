## One-Sentence Summary
Data quality is the measure of how well data meets requirements for accuracy, completeness, reliability, and fitness for use.

## Why This Matters to You
High-quality data is essential for trustworthy analytics, machine learning, and operations. Poor data quality leads to bad decisions, wasted effort, and compliance risks. Managing data quality is a core responsibility in any data-driven organization.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Data quality includes dimensions such as accuracy, completeness, consistency, timeliness, and validity. It is managed through validation, monitoring, and remediation processes.

Quality controls may be automated (e.g., data profiling, anomaly detection) and integrated with governance and lineage systems.

### What It Isn't
Data quality is not a one-time check; it requires continuous monitoring and improvement.

It is also not just about correctness—fitness for purpose matters too.

## How It Works
1. Define quality requirements and metrics.
2. Monitor and validate data against these metrics.
3. Remediate issues and improve processes as needed.

## Think of It Like This
Think of a train inspection process that checks every car for safety and reliability before departure.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You improve trust in analytics and ML outcomes.
- You reduce risk and rework from bad data.
- You support compliance and governance.

**If you don't:**
- Decisions and models are built on unreliable data.
- Compliance and operational risks increase.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are quality metrics and thresholds defined?
- [ ] Is monitoring automated and actionable?
- [ ] Are remediation and feedback loops in place?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Ignoring quality until problems surface downstream.
⚠️ Overly rigid rules that block useful data.
