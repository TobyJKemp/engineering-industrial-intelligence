## One-Sentence Summary
Federated governance is a model where data governance responsibilities are distributed across domain teams, coordinated by central standards and oversight.

## Why This Matters to You
Federated governance enables scalable, agile data management in large organizations. It balances local autonomy with global standards, supporting innovation while ensuring compliance and quality. Without it, data silos and inconsistent practices proliferate.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
In federated governance, domain teams own and manage their data products, while a central group defines policies, standards, and provides oversight. This model is common in data mesh architectures.

### What It Isn't
Federated governance is not a free-for-all; it requires clear standards and coordination.

It is also not purely centralized or decentralized—it is a hybrid.

## How It Works
1. Central group defines policies, standards, and provides tools.
2. Domain teams implement governance locally, aligned to standards.
3. Regular reviews and audits ensure compliance and improvement.

## Think of It Like This
Think of a national railway system where each region manages its own operations, but all follow shared safety and interoperability rules.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- You scale data governance across many teams.
- You enable innovation and local ownership.
- You maintain compliance and quality at scale.

**If you don't:**
- Data silos, inconsistency, and compliance risks increase.
- Central teams become bottlenecks.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Are standards and policies clear and accessible?
- [ ] Are domain teams empowered and accountable?
- [ ] Is oversight and feedback continuous?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Inconsistent practices if standards are unclear.
⚠️ Central bottlenecks or lack of local accountability.
