## One-Sentence Summary
Organizational Sensemaking is the collective process of interpreting and making meaning from organizational information—creating shared understanding that guides action.

## The Core Idea
- **Interpretation:** Organization interprets information
- **Meaning-making:** Creates shared meaning
- **Understanding:** Develops shared understanding
- **Action:** Understanding guides action
- **Learning:** Organization learns through sensemaking

## How It Works
1. **Collect information:** Gather information about situation
2. **Interpret:** What does information mean?
3. **Share:** Share interpretation with others
4. **Align:** Reach shared understanding
5. **Act:** Take action based on understanding

## Think of It Like This
Organizational sensemaking is like **detective work:** Detective collects evidence; interprets what it means; discusses with colleagues; reaches shared understanding; acts based on understanding.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Alignment:** Organization reaches shared understanding
- **Coherence:** Organization operates coherently
- **Learning:** Organization learns from experience
- **Adaptation:** Organization adapts based on understanding

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Shared delusions:** Organization can reach shared false understanding.
⚠️ **Interpretation bias:** Organization's interpretation biased.
