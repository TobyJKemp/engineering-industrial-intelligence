## One-Sentence Summary
Identity and Access Management (IAM) ensures right people have appropriate access to resources—managing user identities, authentication, authorization, and access permissions.

## The Core Idea
- **Identity:** Who is this person/system?
- **Authentication:** Verify identity
- **Authorization:** What can they access?
- **Access control:** Grant/deny access based on authorization
- **Audit:** Track who accessed what

## How It Works
1. **Create identity:** Establish user identity in system
2. **Authenticate:** Verify identity when accessing resources
3. **Determine authorization:** What can this user access?
4. **Control access:** Grant/deny access
5. **Audit:** Log access for audit trail

## Think of It Like This
IAM is like **security badge system**: Badge identifies person; readers authenticate badge; badge grants access to specific doors; log tracks who accessed what.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Security:** Only authorized users access resources
- **Compliance:** Meets compliance requirements
- **Audit:** Track of who accessed what and when
- **Efficiency:** Automated access control

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Access creep:** Users accumulate access over time.
⚠️ **Stale identities:** Accounts for people who left.
