## One-Sentence Summary
Organizational Architecture is the comprehensive design of organizational structure, processes, capabilities, and relationships—ensuring effectiveness and alignment across the organization.

## The Core Idea
- **Holistic:** All organizational elements designed together
- **Structure:** How is organization structured?
- **Processes:** What are key processes?
- **Capabilities:** What capabilities needed?
- **Relationships:** How do elements relate?

## How It Works
1. **Analyze current:** Understand current state
2. **Define future:** Design desired future state
3. **Identify elements:** What elements need designing?
4. **Design each:** Design each element
5. **Integrate:** Ensure elements integrate

## Think of It Like This
Organizational architecture is like **building architecture:** Blueprints show how structure, systems, and spaces work together; guides construction.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Alignment:** All elements aligned
- **Efficiency:** Designed for efficiency
- **Scalability:** Designed for scale
- **Understanding:** Understand how organization works

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Complexity:** Large organizations have complex architecture.
⚠️ **Change lag:** Architecture lags behind reality.
