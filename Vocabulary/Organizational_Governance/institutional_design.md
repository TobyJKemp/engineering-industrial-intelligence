## One-Sentence Summary
Institutional Design is the deliberate structuring of formal rules, incentives, and processes to create organizations that function effectively and adapt to changing environments.

## The Core Idea
- **Rules:** Explicit rules governing behavior
- **Incentives:** Incentives aligned with desired behavior
- **Processes:** Processes implementing rules
- **Evolution:** Design evolves as organization learns

## How It Works
1. **Identify purpose:** What is organization's purpose?
2. **Design rules:** What rules drive desired behavior?
3. **Design incentives:** How do incentives align with purpose?
4. **Design processes:** What processes implement rules?
5. **Evaluate:** How well does design work?

## Think of It Like This
Institutional design is like **sports league rules**: Rules define how games played; incentives for winning; processes for fair competition; rules evolve as needed.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Effectiveness:** Well-designed institutions function effectively
- **Adaptation:** Institutions can adapt to change
- **Alignment:** Rules and incentives aligned
- **Learning:** Institutions learn and improve

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Unintended consequences:** Rules create unexpected behaviors.
⚠️ **Resistance:** People resist institutional change.
