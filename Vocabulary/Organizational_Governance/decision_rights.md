## One-Sentence Summary
Decision Rights explicitly specify who has the right to make particular decisions—providing clarity about decision authority and reducing conflict.

## The Core Idea
- **Explicit rights:** Who decides what is explicit
- **Boundaries:** Clear boundaries on rights
- **Contestable:** Rights can be contested/changed
- **Accountability:** Clear accountability

## How It Works
1. **Identify decisions:** What decisions need rights assigned?
2. **Assign rights:** Who has right to decide?
3. **Define limits:** What are limits on this right?
4. **Document:** Make explicit
5. **Communicate:** Ensure everyone knows

## Think of It Like This
Decision rights are like **property rights**: Clear who owns property; what they can do; what they can't do; clear boundaries.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Clarity:** Eliminates ambiguity about who decides
- **Speed:** Decisions made quickly without escalation
- **Accountability:** Clear who's responsible
- **Avoiding conflict:** Reduces decision conflicts

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Rights conflicts:** Multiple people claim right to same decision.
⚠️ **Authority drift:** Rights change over time without decision.
