## One-Sentence Summary
Shadow IT refers to IT systems deployed and used in an organization without formal approval or governance—creating risks around security, compliance, and integration.

## The Core Idea
- **Unauthorized:** Systems not formally approved
- **Unmanaged:** Not managed by IT department
- **Risky:** Create organizational risks
- **Unintegrated:** Not integrated with official systems
- **Common:** Widespread in most organizations

## How It Works
(How Shadow IT emerges)
1. **Business need:** Team has problem to solve
2. **Slow official channels:** Official process is slow
3. **Quick deployment:** Deploy solution unofficially
4. **Spreading:** Other teams adopt solution
5. **Accumulation:** Shadow systems accumulate

## Think of It Like This
Shadow IT is like **homemade electrical systems:** Homeowner installs outlet without electrician; works fine until fire hazard discovered.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Risk:** Systems may be insecure, unreliable
- **Compliance:** May violate compliance requirements
- **Integration:** Doesn't integrate with official systems
- **Support:** No official support

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Data exposure:** Shadow systems may expose data.
⚠️ **System incompatibility:** Systems incompatible with official systems.
