## One-Sentence Summary
Decision Governance establishes policies and processes for making organizational decisions—ensuring decisions are made at appropriate levels, with right information, and aligned with strategy.

## The Core Idea
- **Decision policies:** Rules for making decisions
- **Authority distribution:** Who decides what?
- **Information flows:** How information flows to decisions
- **Escalation:** When decisions escalate
- **Review:** How decisions are reviewed

## How It Works
1. **Define decision types:** What types of decisions?
2. **Establish process:** How is each type made?
3. **Set authority:** Who makes decision?
4. **Define information needs:** What information needed?
5. **Establish review:** How are decisions reviewed?

## Think of It Like This
Decision governance is like **parliamentary government**: Rules for how decisions are made; who can propose; information sharing; debate; voting.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Alignment:** Decisions aligned with strategy
- **Quality:** Better decisions with right process
- **Consistency:** Consistent decision-making
- **Accountability:** Clear accountability

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Over-process:** Too much process slows decisions.
⚠️ **Under-process:** Insufficient process creates poor decisions.
⚠️ **Misaligned governance:** Process doesn't match decision type.
⚠️ **Opaque process:** People don't understand decision process.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing decision governance:
- [ ] Have you identified decision types and frequency?
- [ ] Are processes defined for each decision type?
- [ ] What information is needed for each process?
- [ ] Who participates in each decision process?
- [ ] Are decision timelines realistic?
- [ ] How do you communicate decisions?
- [ ] Is there feedback on decision quality?
- [ ] How do you handle exceptions?
- [ ] Can decision processes evolve?
- [ ] Are decision processes documented?
- [ ] How do you train people on processes?
- [ ] Is there escalation for difficult decisions?

## Quick Decision Guide

**Implement Decision Governance when:**
- Decision-making is slowing you down
- Decision quality is inconsistent
- Organization is growing and processes are needed
- Alignment or buy-in on decisions is hard

**Keep informal when:**
- Organization is small
- Decisions are simple
- High speed is critical
- Trust is very high
