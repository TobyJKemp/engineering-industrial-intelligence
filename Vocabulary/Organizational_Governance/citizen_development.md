## One-Sentence Summary
Citizen Development empowers non-professional developers to create software and automate processes—democratizing development and enabling rapid application delivery.

## The Core Idea
- **Democratization:** Non-professionals can build
- **Low-code/no-code:** Tools enable building without coding
- **Rapid delivery:** Faster application delivery
- **Governance:** Professional oversight and standards

## How It Works
1. **Enable platforms:** Provide low-code platforms
2. **Train citizens:** Train non-professionals on platforms
3. **Set standards:** Professional standards for citizen development
4. **Govern:** Professional review and oversight

## Think of It Like This
Citizen development is like **blogging:** Enables non-journalists to publish; some good, some bad; but enables rapid content creation.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Speed:** Faster application delivery
- **Cost:** Cheaper than professional developers for simple apps
- **Empowerment:** Enables business users to solve own problems
- **Quality:** Risk of low-quality, unsupportable applications

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Technical debt:** Citizen-built apps may not meet standards.
⚠️ **Shadow IT:** Citizen apps become unsupported systems.
⚠️ **Maintenance burden:** Professional team may need to support poorly-built citizen apps.
⚠️ **Integration issues:** Citizen apps not integrated with enterprise systems.

## Practical Checklist

Before enabling citizen development:
- [ ] Have you selected appropriate low-code/no-code platforms?
- [ ] Are training programs in place for citizen developers?
- [ ] What standards and governance apply to citizen-built apps?
- [ ] How do you prevent shadow IT problems?
- [ ] Is there process for moving citizen apps into production support?
- [ ] Can you manage technical debt from citizen-built apps?
- [ ] How do you handle data governance and security in citizen apps?
- [ ] Is there integration architecture for citizen apps?
- [ ] Can you scale citizen development across organization?
- [ ] What skills do citizen developers need?
- [ ] How do you monitor quality of citizen-built apps?
- [ ] Is there escalation path when citizen apps become business-critical?

## Quick Decision Guide

**Enable Citizen Development when:**
- You have high volume of simple automation needs
- Business users understand their requirements clearly
- You have adequate governance and support structures
- Low-code platforms fit your architecture

**Be cautious when:**
- Organization has poor IT governance
- Applications are business-critical or highly regulated
- Your technical support team is already overloaded
