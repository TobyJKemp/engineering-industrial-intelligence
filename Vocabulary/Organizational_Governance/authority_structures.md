## One-Sentence Summary
Authority Structures define who has power to make which decisions—determining the distribution of decision-making power throughout the organization.

## The Core Idea
- **Decision authority:** Who can make what decisions?
- **Boundaries:** Clear boundaries on authority
- **Delegation:** How is authority delegated?
- **Escalation:** When must decisions escalate?

## How It Works
1. **Define decisions:** What major decisions must be made?
2. **Assign authority:** Who makes each decision?
3. **Set bounds:** What are limits on authority?
4. **Define escalation:** When do decisions escalate?

## Think of It Like This
Authority structures are like **military chain of command**: Clear who has authority; limits on that authority; escalation paths defined.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Clarity:** Clear who can decide
- **Speed:** Decisions made quickly without unnecessary escalation
- **Accountability:** Clear accountability for decisions
- **Alignment:** Decisions aligned with organizational strategy

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Unclear authority:** Creates confusion and conflict.
⚠️ **Authority drift:** Authority shifts over time without explicit decision.
⚠️ **Excessive centralization:** All authority at top creates bottlenecks.
⚠️ **Excessive decentralization:** Too much local authority creates inconsistency.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing authority structures:
- [ ] Have you defined major decision types and who decides each?
- [ ] Are authority boundaries clear and documented?
- [ ] What escalation paths exist when authority is exceeded?
- [ ] How do you handle decisions that cross authority boundaries?
- [ ] Are authority limits communicated and understood?
- [ ] Can authority adapt as organization evolves?
- [ ] Is there mechanism for delegating authority?
- [ ] How do you prevent unauthorized decisions?
- [ ] Are there consequences for exceeding authority?
- [ ] How frequently do you review authority structures?
- [ ] Is authority distributed appropriately for speed vs. control?
- [ ] Can you trace decisions to authority holders?

## Quick Decision Guide

**Implement authority structures when:**
- Organization has grown beyond informal decision-making
- You need clear speed of decisions
- Decisions have significant financial or strategic impact
- Compliance requires clear authority trails

**Revisit authority when:**
- Organization structure changes
- Decision patterns reveal bottlenecks
- New decision types emerge
