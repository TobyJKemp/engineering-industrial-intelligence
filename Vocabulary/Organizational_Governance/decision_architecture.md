## One-Sentence Summary
Decision Architecture is the overall design of how an organization makes decisions—including decision structures, processes, authorities, and information flows.

## The Core Idea
- **Decision structures:** How are decision processes organized?
- **Information flows:** How does information flow to decisions?
- **Authority distribution:** How is decision authority distributed?
- **Escalation:** How are escalations handled?

## How It Works
1. **Map decisions:** What are major decision types?
2. **Design processes:** What's process for each decision type?
3. **Establish information flows:** How does information flow?
4. **Assign authority:** Who makes what decisions?
5. **Test:** Validate that architecture works

## Think of It Like This
Decision architecture is like **court system architecture**: Different court levels for different cases; appeal processes; information flows; authority clearly defined.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Alignment:** Decisions aligned with strategy
- **Speed:** Quick decisions when designed well
- **Quality:** Better decisions with right information
- **Understanding:** Can see how organization makes decisions

## Watch Out For

⚠️ **Bottlenecks:** Poor design creates decision bottlenecks.
⚠️ **Silos:** Information silos prevent good decisions.
⚠️ **Static design:** Architecture doesn't evolve with changing needs.
⚠️ **Complexity:** Architecture becomes too complex to understand or maintain.

## Practical Checklist

Before designing decision architecture:
- [ ] Have you mapped key decisions in your organization?
- [ ] Do you understand decision dependencies?
- [ ] What information is needed for each decision?
- [ ] How quickly do decisions need to be made?
- [ ] Who should make each decision?
- [ ] Are there decision escalation paths?
- [ ] Can architecture scale with organization growth?
- [ ] How do you handle decision changes?
- [ ] Are decisions documented and communicated?
- [ ] Is there feedback on decision quality?
- [ ] Can architecture adapt to new types of decisions?
- [ ] How do you measure decision effectiveness?

## Quick Decision Guide

**Design Decision Architecture when:**
- Organization has complex decision-making
- Decisions are interdependent
- You need consistent decision-making
- Organization is growing rapidly

**Keep simple when:**
- Organization is small
- Decisions are mostly independent
- People know how to make decisions
