## One-Sentence Summary
Operational Cognition is the organization's collective ability to perceive, understand, learn, and reason about its operations—enabling adaptive decision-making and improvement.

## The Core Idea
- **Perception:** Organization perceives what's happening
- **Understanding:** Organization understands what it perceives
- **Learning:** Organization learns from experience
- **Reasoning:** Organization reasons about decisions
- **Adaptation:** Organization adapts based on learning

## How It Works
1. **Perception systems:** Collect data about operations
2. **Understanding:** Analyze data to understand patterns
3. **Learning systems:** Extract insights and lessons
4. **Reasoning:** Use insights to reason about decisions
5. **Adaptation:** Adapt operations based on learning

## Think of It Like This
Operational cognition is like **human cognition in business context**: Person perceives situation; understands it; learns from experience; reasons about decisions; adapts behavior.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Adaptation:** Organization adapts to changing environment
- **Learning:** Organization learns and improves
- **Resilience:** Organization responds to problems
- **Innovation:** Organization innovates based on learning

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **False learning:** Learning something that's wrong.
⚠️ **Adaptation lag:** Organization slow to adapt.
