## One-Sentence Summary
Responsibility Assignment explicitly assigns who is responsible for what outcomes—creating clear accountability and enabling effective management.

## The Core Idea
- **Clear assignment:** Who is responsible for what?
- **Accountability:** Clear accountability for outcomes
- **Authority:** Authority matched to responsibility
- **Communication:** Everyone knows responsibilities
- **Enforcement:** Responsibilities enforced

## How It Works
1. **Identify outcomes:** What outcomes need accountability?
2. **Assign responsibility:** Who is responsible?
3. **Specify authority:** What authority does person have?
4. **Communicate:** Tell everyone
5. **Enforce:** Hold responsible for outcomes

## Think of It Like This
Responsibility assignment is like **sports team rosters:** Each player has position; knows what they're responsible for; held accountable for performance.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Clarity:** Everyone knows who's responsible
- **Accountability:** People held accountable
- **Speed:** Don't waste time debating who's responsible
- **Results:** Clear responsibility drives results

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Diffusion of responsibility:** Too many people responsible.
⚠️ **Accountability gap:** Nobody takes responsibility.
