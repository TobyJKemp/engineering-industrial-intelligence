## One-Sentence Summary
Federated Governance structures governance decisions between central and local authorities—with both levels having clear decision rights and coordination mechanisms.

## The Core Idea
- **Shared authority:** Central and local both have authority
- **Clear boundaries:** Clear what's central vs. local
- **Coordination:** Mechanisms ensuring coordination
- **Autonomy:** Local units have autonomy

## How It Works
1. **Define authorities:** What's central? What's local?
2. **Establish processes:** How do central and local coordinate?
3. **Set standards:** Central sets minimum standards
4. **Allow flexibility:** Local can exceed standards
5. **Monitor:** Ensure compliance and coordination

## Think of It Like This
Federated governance is like **Canadian government**: Central government on some matters; provinces on others; both have authority; coordination mechanisms.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Scalability:** Can scale while maintaining standards
- **Autonomy:** Local units have autonomy
- **Coherence:** Maintains organizational coherence
- **Complexity:** Complex to coordinate

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Authority conflicts:** Unclear boundaries create conflict.
⚠️ **Coordination failures:** Coordination breaks down.
