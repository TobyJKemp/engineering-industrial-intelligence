## One-Sentence Summary
Shadow AI refers to AI systems deployed and used in an organization without formal approval or governance—creating risks around quality, security, and compliance.

## The Core Idea
- **Unauthorized:** AI systems not formally approved
- **Unmanaged:** Systems not managed by IT/governance
- **Risky:** Systems pose risk to organization
- **Unmonitored:** Activities not monitored
- **Problematic:** Can cause organizational problems

## How It Works
(How Shadow AI emerges)
1. **Business need:** Team has need for AI capability
2. **Fast deployment:** Deploy AI quickly without approval
3. **Operation:** System operates outside governance
4. **Risk:** Risks accumulate
5. **Discovery:** Organization eventually discovers system

## Think of It Like This
Shadow AI is like **shadow IT:** Teams deploy unsupported systems to solve problems quickly; creates technical debt and risk.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Risk:** Systems may be insecure, untrustworthy
- **Compliance:** May violate compliance requirements
- **Data:** May misuse organizational data
- **Integration:** Not integrated with other systems

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Pervasiveness:** Shadow AI widespread in organizations.
⚠️ **Discovery difficulty:** Hard to find all shadow AI.
