## One-Sentence Summary
Organizational Governance is the system of structures, processes, and controls that direct and oversee the organization—ensuring alignment with strategy and managing risk.

## The Core Idea
- **Direction:** Governance provides direction to organization
- **Oversight:** Governance oversees organizational operations
- **Controls:** Governance establishes controls
- **Accountability:** Governance establishes accountability
- **Compliance:** Governance ensures compliance

## How It Works
1. **Define governance:** What is governance framework?
2. **Establish direction:** How does governance provide direction?
3. **Set controls:** What controls does governance establish?
4. **Assign accountability:** Who is accountable for what?
5. **Monitor:** Monitor governance effectiveness

## Think of It Like This
Organizational governance is like **ship's command structure:** Captain provides direction; officers execute; clear accountability; processes ensure ship operates effectively.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Direction:** Organization knows where it's going
- **Risk:** Reduces organizational risk
- **Accountability:** Clear accountability
- **Performance:** Governance can improve performance

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Governance theater:** Governance that doesn't actually govern.
⚠️ **Too restrictive:** Governance that stifles organization.
