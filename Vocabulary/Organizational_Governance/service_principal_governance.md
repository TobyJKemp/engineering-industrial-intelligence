## One-Sentence Summary
Service Principal Governance manages non-human identities (service principals, service accounts) that perform automated tasks—ensuring they have appropriate access and are properly monitored.

## The Core Idea
- **Non-human identities:** Applications and services need identities
- **Access control:** Control what service principals can access
- **Monitoring:** Monitor service principal activities
- **Credentials:** Manage service principal credentials
- **Least privilege:** Grant minimum needed access

## How It Works
1. **Create principal:** Create service principal for application
2. **Assign permissions:** Grant needed permissions
3. **Configure credentials:** Set up credentials (keys, certificates)
4. **Monitor:** Monitor principal activities
5. **Audit:** Audit access and activities

## Think of It Like This
Service principal governance is like **security clearances:** Person needs clearance to access information; government monitors use; revokes if misused.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Security:** Prevents unauthorized access
- **Audit:** Track service principal activities
- **Compliance:** Meets compliance requirements
- **Control:** Control what applications can do

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Credential sprawl:** Credentials scattered everywhere.
⚠️ **Stale principals:** Service principals never cleaned up.
