## One-Sentence Summary
Assumption Management is systematically identifying, documenting, and validating organizational assumptions—preventing decisions based on unexamined false assumptions.

## The Core Idea
- **Hidden assumptions:** Most organizations operate on unstated assumptions
- **Risk:** False assumptions lead to bad decisions
- **Validation:** Assumptions should be tested
- **Governance:** Process for managing assumptions

## How It Works
1. **Identify assumptions:** What are we assuming?
2. **Document:** Make assumptions explicit
3. **Validate:** Test assumptions; are they true?
4. **Update:** Change assumptions if proven false
5. **Monitor:** Keep monitoring key assumptions

## Think of It Like This
Assumption management is like **aircraft pre-flight check**: Pilot doesn't assume aircraft is ready; systematically checks assumptions about aircraft status.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Prevents failure:** False assumptions often cause failure
- **Decisions:** Better decisions based on validated assumptions
- **Risk:** Reduces risk of assumption-based failures
- **Learning:** Validates understanding of business

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing assumption management:
- [ ] Have you identified key assumptions underlying your strategy?
- [ ] Are assumptions documented and tracked?
- [ ] Is there regular review of assumptions against reality?
- [ ] Who is responsible for monitoring each assumption?
- [ ] What triggers would invalidate each assumption?
- [ ] How quickly can you pivot if assumptions prove wrong?
- [ ] Are assumptions communicated to all stakeholders?
- [ ] Is there escalation when assumptions become questionable?
- [ ] Do you track which decisions depend on which assumptions?
- [ ] How do you manage hidden or implicit assumptions?
- [ ] Are assumptions updated as new information emerges?
- [ ] Is there post-mortems on assumptions that proved wrong?

## Watch Out For

⚠️ **Hidden assumptions:** Unstated assumptions that team members don't share.
⚠️ **Assumption drift:** Assumptions change over time without explicit decision.
⚠️ **Late discovery:** Assumptions fail, but failure discovered too late to pivot.
⚠️ **Over-confidence:** Assuming things without adequate evidence or testing.

## Quick Decision Guide

**Use Assumption Management when:**
- Making decisions based on forecasts or predictions
- Strategy depends on market or organizational changes
- You need to track dependencies and relationships

**Review assumptions when:**
- New information emerges about key factors
- Project results differ significantly from expectations
- Market conditions change significantly
