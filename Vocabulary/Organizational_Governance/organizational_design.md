## One-Sentence Summary
Organizational Design is deliberately structuring an organization—including its hierarchy, roles, processes, and systems—to achieve strategic objectives efficiently.

## The Core Idea
- **Strategic:** Design aligned with strategy
- **Structure:** Design hierarchy and reporting
- **Roles:** Define roles and responsibilities
- **Processes:** Define key processes
- **Systems:** Design supporting systems

## How It Works
1. **Understand strategy:** What strategy is organization pursuing?
2. **Identify requirements:** What's required to execute?
3. **Design structure:** Design hierarchy and roles
4. **Design processes:** Design key processes
5. **Implement:** Implement design

## Think of It Like This
Organizational design is like **user interface design:** Designer considers purpose, then structures interface elements to achieve that purpose efficiently.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Effectiveness:** Well-designed organizations execute strategy
- **Efficiency:** Design can improve efficiency
- **Clarity:** Clear roles and responsibilities
- **Alignment:** Strategy and structure aligned

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Oversimplification:** Organizational design is complex.
⚠️ **Resistance to change:** People resist organizational change.
