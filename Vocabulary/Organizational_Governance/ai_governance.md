## One-Sentence Summary
AI Governance establishes policies, controls, and decision-making frameworks for AI systems—ensuring appropriate use, managing risks, and maintaining alignment with organizational values.

## The Core Idea
- **Policy framework:** Rules governing AI system use
- **Risk management:** Identifying and managing AI-specific risks
- **Decision rights:** Who decides about AI systems?
- **Compliance:** Ensuring regulatory compliance

## How It Works
1. **Define AI strategy:** What role do AI systems play?
2. **Establish policies:** Rules for AI development and use
3. **Identify risks:** What AI risks exist?
4. **Set controls:** Controls managing identified risks
5. **Assign responsibility:** Who's responsible for governance?

## Think of It Like This
AI governance is like **pharmaceutical governance**: Policies on drug development, testing, approval, monitoring; risk management; responsibility assignment.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Risk management:** Manages AI-specific risks
- **Compliance:** Ensures regulatory compliance
- **Trust:** Builds organizational trust in AI systems
- **Strategy:** Aligns AI systems with strategy

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Over-governance:** Too much control stifles innovation and slows AI deployment.
⚠️ **Under-governance:** Insufficient control creates risks and compliance issues.
⚠️ **Static policies:** AI governance must evolve as technology and threats change.
⚠️ **Complexity:** Over-complicated governance prevents adoption and creates shadow systems.

## Practical Checklist

Before implementing AI governance:
- [ ] Have you defined AI governance policies and decision rights?
- [ ] What risks have you identified for your AI systems?
- [ ] Are controls in place to manage identified risks?
- [ ] Who is responsible for AI governance decisions?
- [ ] Is there compliance monitoring and reporting?
- [ ] How do you handle AI system transparency and explainability?
- [ ] Are there controls on model drift and performance degradation?
- [ ] What data governance applies to AI training data?
- [ ] How do you ensure responsible AI principles are followed?
- [ ] Is there escalation path for governance violations?
- [ ] Can AI governance scale with your organization's growth?
- [ ] How frequently do you review and update AI policies?

## Quick Decision Guide

**Implement AI Governance when:**
- You're deploying AI systems in your organization
- You have regulatory or compliance requirements for AI
- You want to manage AI-related risks proactively
- You need clear accountability for AI system outcomes

**Consider phased approach when:**
- Organization is new to AI
- Resources for governance are limited
- Start with risk-based governance, expand over time
