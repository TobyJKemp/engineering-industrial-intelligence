## One-Sentence Summary
Distributed Governance decentralizes governance decisions—with local units having significant governance authority while maintaining overall organizational alignment.

## The Core Idea
- **Decentralized:** Governance decisions made locally
- **Aligned:** Local governance aligned with overall strategy
- **Coherent:** Mechanisms maintaining organizational coherence
- **Autonomous:** Units operate with autonomy

## How It Works
1. **Define what's centralized:** What governance remains central?
2. **Define what's distributed:** What governance is distributed?
3. **Establish guidelines:** Guidelines for local governance
4. **Create coordination:** Mechanisms for coordination
5. **Monitor:** Ensure alignment

## Think of It Like This
Distributed governance is like **federal system of government**: Central government on some matters; states govern on others; clear separation of powers.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Responsiveness:** Local units can respond quickly
- **Innovation:** Local innovation in governance
- **Autonomy:** Units have autonomy
- **Complexity:** Complex to coordinate

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Fragmentation:** Local units drift apart.
⚠️ **Coordination costs:** High coordination overhead.
