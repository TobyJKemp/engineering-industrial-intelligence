## One-Sentence Summary
Capability Procurement is the process of acquiring capabilities (skills, services, tools) from external markets—whether agents, contractors, or services—to meet organizational needs.

## The Core Idea
- **Market search:** Find capabilities available in market
- **Evaluation:** Assess quality, fit, cost
- **Selection:** Choose best option
- **Integration:** Integrate into operations

## How It Works
1. **Define capability needed:** What do we need?
2. **Market search:** What's available?
3. **Evaluate options:** Cost, quality, fit
4. **Negotiate:** Terms and pricing
5. **Integrate:** Bring into operations

## Think of It Like This
Capability procurement is like **hiring contractor**: Need specific skill; find contractor with that skill; negotiate terms; integrate into team.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Speed:** Get capability quickly vs. building
- **Flexibility:** Can acquire/release as needed
- **Cost:** Often cheaper than internal build
- **Risk:** External dependencies

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Integration difficulty:** External capability may not integrate well.
⚠️ **Hidden costs:** Implementation and integration costs.
⚠️ **Quality mismatch:** Capability doesn't match actual needs.
⚠️ **Lock-in:** Difficult to switch to alternative once integrated.

## Practical Checklist

Before procuring external capability:
- [ ] Have you clearly specified capability needed?
- [ ] Have you searched market and identified options?
- [ ] Have you evaluated each option thoroughly?
- [ ] What's total cost including integration?
- [ ] Is quality/maturity of capability adequate?
- [ ] Can capability integrate with existing infrastructure?
- [ ] Is vendor support adequate?
- [ ] Are terms flexible enough for your needs?
- [ ] Have you negotiated favorable terms?
- [ ] Can you measure success of capability?
