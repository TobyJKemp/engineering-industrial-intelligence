# Service-Oriented Organizations

## One-Sentence Summary
Service-Oriented Organizations structure around services provided to customers—with internal components designed to deliver services efficiently.

## The Core Idea
- **Customer focus:** Organization design starts with customer needs
- **Service definition:** Clear service definitions
- **Service teams:** Teams organized around services
- **Service interfaces:** Clear interfaces between services

## How It Works
1. **Define services:** What services do we provide?
2. **Design service delivery:** How will services be delivered?
3. **Organize for services:** Structure teams to deliver services
4. **Manage interfaces:** Manage interactions between services

## Think of It Like This
Service-oriented organizations are like **airline organization**: Organized around services (flights, ground services, maintenance); each service clearly defined and delivered.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Customer focus:** Organization aligned with customer needs
- **Clarity:** Clear service boundaries
- **Efficiency:** Organized to deliver services efficiently
- **Scalability:** Can add services without major reorganization

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Service duplication:** Services may duplicate across teams.
⚠️ **Service dependencies:** Services may depend on other services.
⚠️ **Silos:** Service orientation can create organizational silos.
⚠️ **Boundary disputes:** Unclear which service is responsible for what.

## Practical Checklist

Before restructuring around services:
- [ ] Have you identified all services you provide?
- [ ] Are service boundaries clear and non-overlapping?
- [ ] Can you organize teams around services?
- [ ] Are service definitions customer-focused?
- [ ] Can you manage dependencies between services?
- [ ] Is technology architecture service-oriented?
- [ ] Can services scale independently?
- [ ] Are service level agreements defined?
- [ ] Can you measure service delivery?
- [ ] Will service organization improve customer satisfaction?
