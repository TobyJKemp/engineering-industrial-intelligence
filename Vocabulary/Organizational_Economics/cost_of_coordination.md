# Cost of Coordination

## At a Glance
| | |
|---|---|

## One-Sentence Summary
Cost of Coordination is the overhead required to coordinate activities—including communication, decision-making, and conflict resolution—which affects whether activities are kept together or separated.

## The Core Idea
- **Coordination overhead:** Communication, meetings, decisions cost time/money
- **Trade-off:** High coordination cost means activities should be separated
- **Low cost:** Low coordination cost means activities can be combined
- **Economies:** Determines organizational structure

## How It Works
1. **Identify activities:** What activities do we do?
2. **Calculate coordination:** How much coordination required?
3. **Calculate coordination cost:** What's overhead?
4. **Decide organization:** Organize to minimize cost

## Think of It Like This
Coordination cost is like **shipping: If shipping costs high, keep manufacturing and sales together. If shipping cheap, can separate.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Structure decisions:** Coordination cost drives organizational structure
- **Efficiency:** Minimize coordination overhead
- **Optimization:** Find structure minimizing coordination cost
- **Change:** As coordination tools improve, structure can change

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Hidden costs:** Coordination costs often hidden; not counted.
⚠️ **Technology changes:** New coordination tools can change structure.
⚠️ **Incomplete costing:** Indirect coordination costs often missed.
⚠️ **False economy:** Lowering coordination cost may harm quality.

## Practical Checklist

Before organizing activities by coordination cost:
- [ ] Have you identified all coordination activities?
- [ ] Have you estimated coordination costs (meetings, decisions, communication)?
- [ ] Are you counting all direct and indirect costs?
- [ ] What's cost of bringing activities together?
- [ ] What's cost of separating activities?
- [ ] How sensitive is structure to coordination cost changes?
- [ ] Could new tools lower coordination costs?
- [ ] Are quality implications understood?
- [ ] Can you measure coordination cost accurately?
- [ ] Is organizational structure optimizing for wrong metric?
