## One-Sentence Summary
Digital Contractors are AI agents hired for specific work, with defined scope and duration—functioning like human contractors but operating continuously and without employment overhead.

## The Core Idea
- **Project-based:** Hired for specific work
- **Defined scope:** Clear what they will/won't do
- **Defined duration:** Specific end date
- **No overhead:** No benefits, taxes, HR overhead

## How It Works
1. **Define work:** What specific work needed?
2. **Hire contractor:** Select and contract with agent
3. **Specify terms:** Scope, duration, performance standards
4. **Work:** Agent does work
5. **Release:** End contract when done

## Think of It Like This
Digital contractors are like **human contractors**: Hire for specific project; defined scope and timeline; no permanent employment.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Flexibility:** Add capacity for specific work
- **Cost:** No employment overhead
- **Specialization:** Specialized contractor for specialized work
- **Scalability:** Easy to add/remove contractors

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Continuity:** No institutional memory; each new project starts fresh.
⚠️ **Quality:** Must manage contractor quality.
⚠️ **Integration overhead:** Onboarding contractor for short project may be expensive.
⚠️ **Knowledge loss:** Contractor knowledge leaves when project ends.

## Practical Checklist

Before hiring digital contractors:
- [ ] Is work clearly scoped and project-bounded?
- [ ] Do you have standardized contractor onboarding?
- [ ] Can you verify contractor capability?
- [ ] Are performance standards defined?
- [ ] Is contract price fair for work?
- [ ] Can you integrate contractor into workflow?
- [ ] Is knowledge captured during project?
- [ ] Do you have mechanism for contractor replacement if needed?
- [ ] Are dependencies on contractor minimized?
- [ ] Can you transition work smoothly when contractor leaves?
