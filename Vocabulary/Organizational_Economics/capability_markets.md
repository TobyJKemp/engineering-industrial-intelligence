## One-Sentence Summary
Capability Markets are economic systems where organizations can acquire capabilities (skills, functions, services) from market rather than building internally—enabling focus on core capabilities.

## The Core Idea
- **Make-or-buy:** Decide whether to build or buy capability
- **Market price:** Capability price set by supply/demand
- **Specialization:** External specialists provide specialized capabilities
- **Focus:** Organization focuses on core; buys rest

## How It Works
1. **Identify capability gap:** What capability needed?
2. **Market assessment:** Can we buy it? What's cost?
3. **Make/buy decision:** Build or buy?
4. **Acquisition:** Buy from market if economical

## Think of It Like This
Capability markets are like **outsourcing services**: Company needs legal services; buys from law firm rather than building internal legal department.

## The "So What?" Factor
- **Efficiency:** Buy specialized services cheaper than building
- **Focus:** Keep resources focused on core business
- **Flexibility:** Can acquire/release capabilities as needed
- **Specialization:** Access to specialized expertise

## Watch Out For
⚠️ **Loss of capability:** If capabilities always outsourced, may lose capability.
⚠️ **Vendor dependence:** Dependence on external vendors.
⚠️ **Market volatility:** Capability prices can fluctuate.
⚠️ **Quality variable:** Market capabilities of variable quality.

## Practical Checklist

Before sourcing capabilities from market:
- [ ] Is this a non-core capability?
- [ ] Can you evaluate market options?
- [ ] What's competitive market price?
- [ ] Can you assess quality of market capabilities?
- [ ] What happens if market disappears?
- [ ] Is core competency at risk?
- [ ] Do you need strategic control of capability?
- [ ] Can you integrate external capability?
- [ ] Is there in-house expertise to manage vendors?
- [ ] Can you maintain optionality to build internally?
