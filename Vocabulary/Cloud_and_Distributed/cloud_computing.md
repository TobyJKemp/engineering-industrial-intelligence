## One-Sentence Summary
Cloud computing delivers compute, storage, and managed services on demand over networks with elastic scaling and usage-based pricing.

## Why This Matters to You
Cloud computing changes how quickly teams can build and ship systems. Instead of waiting on hardware procurement, you can provision infrastructure in minutes. It also shifts operational models toward automation and managed services. Done well, cloud increases speed and resilience; done poorly, it increases spend and complexity.

## The Core Idea
### What It Is
Cloud providers expose infrastructure and platform capabilities as services: IaaS, PaaS, and serverless options. Teams consume resources through APIs, consoles, and infrastructure-as-code.

Elasticity, global footprint, and managed services are the core value drivers.

### What It Isn't
It is not automatically cheaper than on-premises. It is also not a substitute for architecture discipline or governance.

## How It Works
1. Workloads are provisioned in provider-managed datacenters.
2. Resources are scaled and monitored through policy and automation.
3. Billing tracks actual consumption and reserved commitments.

## Think of It Like This
Using a rail network instead of building your own tracks for every route: you focus on service delivery, not infrastructure ownership.

## The "So What?" Factor
**If you use this:**
- Faster experimentation and delivery.
- Access to advanced managed capabilities.
- Better elasticity for changing demand.

**If you don't:**
- Slower provisioning and release cycles.
- Higher fixed-cost infrastructure burden.

## Practical Checklist
Before implementing, ask yourself:
- [ ] Which service model fits each workload?
- [ ] What governance controls prevent cost and risk drift?
- [ ] How will reliability and security be validated continuously?

## Watch Out For
⚠️ Vendor lock-in without portability planning.
⚠️ Cost sprawl from unmanaged resources and poor lifecycle controls.
