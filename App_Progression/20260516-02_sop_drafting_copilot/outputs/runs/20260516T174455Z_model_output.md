## Summary
This SOP standardizes cleaning and startup verification for photoelectric sensors on conveyor lane B.  
It aims to reduce contamination-related stoppages and prevent carton misrouting at morning startup.

## Scope
- Applies to **line operators** and **shift leads** on the food packaging line.
- Covers **sensor cleaning, startup verification, recordkeeping, and escalation** before restarting lane B after overnight shutdown.

## Prerequisites
- You must be trained on:
  - **SOP-LOTO-12** (lockout procedure)
  - **WI-CLEAN-04** (approved cleaning material)
  - Andon escalation process (Level 2)
- Required materials:
  - Approved solvent pads per WI-CLEAN-04
  - Lint-free wipes
  - PPE required by area rules
  - Startup verification checklist form (paper or digital log)

## Procedure Steps
1. **Apply lockout before contact with sensors.** *(Estimated time: 3 minutes)*  
   Follow **SOP-LOTO-12** and confirm zero-energy state for conveyor lane B before touching sensors or nearby moving parts.

2. **Inspect each lane B photoelectric sensor for visible residue and damage.** *(Estimated time: 2 minutes)*  
   Check lens surface, mounting bracket, and cable condition. Record any crack, loose mount, or frayed cable on the checklist.

3. **Clean each sensor lens using only approved material.** *(Estimated time: 4 minutes)*  
   Use solvent pads listed in **WI-CLEAN-04**. Wipe lens with one-direction strokes, then dry with a lint-free wipe. Do not use dry wiping alone.

4. **Verify sensor alignment and mounting stability.** *(Estimated time: 2 minutes)*  
   Confirm sensor points at target zone and does not shift when lightly pressed. Tighten mount if adjustment is allowed by setup standard.

5. **Remove lockout and restore power per SOP-LOTO-12.** *(Estimated time: 3 minutes)*  
   Clear tools and wipes from conveyor area before re-energizing.

6. **Run startup verification test with no cartons present.** *(Estimated time: 2 minutes)*  
   Observe sensor status indicators for 10 continuous cycles. Confirm no false trigger during empty-belt movement.

7. **Run startup verification test with test cartons.** *(Estimated time: 3 minutes)*  
   Send 5 test cartons through lane B. Confirm each trigger matches carton position and no missed or extra counts occur.

8. **Confirm routing response at downstream decision point.** *(Estimated time: 2 minutes)*  
   Verify test cartons follow expected route and no misroute alarm occurs.

9. **Record completion and results before production release.** *(Estimated time: 2 minutes)*  
   Log cleaning method, test results, operator name, time, and shift lead sign-off in the startup checklist.

10. **Release lane B to production only after all checks pass.** *(Estimated time: 1 minute)*  
   Start normal operation after checklist completion and shift lead approval.

## Safety and Quality Checks
### Mandatory Controls (Must)
- You **must** apply and remove lockout using **SOP-LOTO-12**.
- You **must** use only cleaning materials defined in **WI-CLEAN-04**.
- You **must** complete both empty-belt and 5-carton verification tests.
- You **must** complete recordkeeping fields for traceability before line release.
- This procedure is a **Critical Control Point** for startup quality.

### Recommended Practices (Should)
- You **should** replace wipes/pads between sensors to avoid cross-transfer of residue.
- You **should** ask a second operator to observe test carton routing during verification.
- You **should** review prior-shift sensor issues before starting checks.

## Escalation
Escalate to **Shift Lead via Andon Level 2** immediately if any of the following occur:
- Sensor false trigger appears **1 or more times** during the 10-cycle empty-belt test.
- Any missed trigger or extra trigger occurs during the 5-carton test.
- Sensor lens remains visibly contaminated after **2 cleaning attempts**.
- Any physical damage is found (cracked lens, loose mount not correctable, cable damage).
- Routing error occurs for **1 or more** test cartons.

Shift Lead actions:
- Hold startup release for lane B.
- Open incident record and notify maintenance/quality as required by site process.
- Authorize restart only after corrective action and repeat verification pass.
